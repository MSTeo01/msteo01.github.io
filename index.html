About me

My name is Ming and I'm a Data Engineer at Quantexa . I grew up in Malacca, Malaysia and received my International General Certificate of Secondary Education Diploma from Kolej Tuanku Ja'afar in 2019. I then went on to attend De Anza College and UC Berkeley where I graduated with a degree in Data Science. Recently, I completed the Data Engineer Academy at Quantexa, where I was onboarded and trained to develop a deep technical understanding of the ETL process and software design. I gained comprehensive skills in how to develop, debug, and customise the Quantexa Platform and its features. At Nicholas C. Petris Center , I designed streamlined healthcare data schemas to simplify analyses load of different models, built a Python pipeline to process hospital operations data, and optimized healthcare database performance. This helped reduce analyses times from ~2 weeks to ~4 days and was a key component in prompting California healthcare policy changes.

Academics

Growing up it wasn't clear what drove me intellectually, but I find logic/pattern-matching puzzles to be fascinating and curious. As advised by close ones, I figured I'd give Computer Science a shot until I took an Intro to Statistics course. That course catalyzed my love for data; it was spectacular to the point where I dove deeper to find out there was another field that combines both statistics and computing to work with data. As such, I came to Berkeley intending to major in Data Science. My love for data was further amplified when I took Data 8 my junior fall. That course, which I spent a semester helping out students with its concept, brought me to a new world of interdisciplinary computing and data; it was amazing how many doors were opened after I learned to harness the power of code. After completing all the lower-division Data/Math/CS courses, my love for pattern-decluttering/pattern-matching only grew fonder after I saw how entangled the sub-fields of data science are. Joe Reis's Fundamentals of Data Engineering proved infinitely valuable in my later coursework, particularly in Yan's offering of DATA101.
